CCTV Cable vs Normal Cable: A Technical Comparison

Choosing the correct cable is critical for any CCTV surveillance system. A common mistake is using normal electrical cables instead of dedicated CCTV cables, which leads to signal loss, noise interference, and frequent system failures. This article explains the technical differences between CCTV cables and normal cables, helping system integrators, factories, and warehouses make the right decision.

What is a CCTV Cable?

A CCTV cable is specially engineered for video surveillance systems. It is designed to transmit high-frequency video signals with minimal attenuation while maintaining signal integrity over long distances.

  • Pure copper or copper-clad conductors
  • 75-ohm impedance (for coaxial CCTV cables)
  • Shielding layers to prevent EMI/RFI interference
  • UV-resistant and flame-retardant insulation

What is a Normal Electrical Cable?

Normal electrical cables are designed only for power transmission. They are not built to carry high-frequency video signals and lack shielding, impedance control, and signal protection.

  • Designed for AC/DC power only
  • No impedance matching
  • Minimal or no shielding
  • Higher signal loss for video transmission

Why Normal Cables Fail in CCTV Installations

Using normal cables in CCTV systems causes:

  • Blurry or distorted video
  • Frequent signal drop
  • Electrical noise and interference
  • Higher maintenance cost
  • Shorter system lifespan

No. Normal cables are not designed for video transmission and cause signal degradation and interference.

What cable is best for long-distance CCTV?

High-quality CCTV coaxial or structured CCTV cables with proper shielding are best for long distances.
