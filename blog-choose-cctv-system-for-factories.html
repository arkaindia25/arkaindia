How to Choose the Right CCTV System for Factories & Warehouses

Factories and warehouses require a higher level of security compared to homes or small offices. Large areas, valuable assets, and continuous operations make it essential to install the right CCTV system. Choosing the wrong setup can result in blind spots and security risks.

Understand Your Security Requirements

Start by identifying critical areas such as entry and exit points, storage zones, production floors, loading bays, and perimeter boundaries. This assessment helps determine camera placement and quantity.

Storage and Recording Capacity

Warehouses generate large volumes of video data. Choose storage solutions based on the number of cameras, recording duration, and future expansion requirements.

Outdoor & Environmental Protection

Ensure cameras and cables are resistant to dust, moisture, heat, and vibration. Weatherproof equipment increases system life and performance.

Monitoring and Compliance

Modern CCTV systems should allow remote monitoring, mobile access, and compliance with safety and security regulations. These features provide real-time visibility and operational control.
